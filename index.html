Participant Information and Informed Consent

Study: Effectiveness of WhatsApp Communities and Groups in Supporting Learning and Communication at T. A. Marryshow Community College
Principal Investigator: Christopher Mignon (Lecturer, School of Applied Arts & Technology)
Institution: T. A. Marryshow Community College
Contact: christopherm@tamcc.edu.gd

1. Purpose

You are invited to participate in a research study examining the use, perceived effectiveness, and affordances of WhatsApp groups and a WhatsApp Community for academic communication and learning support at T. A. Marryshow Community College. The objective is to identify strengths, limitations, and recommendations for pedagogical use of mobile messaging platforms.

2. Procedures

If you consent to participate, you will be asked to complete an anonymous online questionnaire that requires approximately 7–12 minutes. Participation in follow-up interviews or focus groups is optional and will be described separately; such follow-up activities will require a separate consent process.

3. Eligibility and Minimum Age

Participation is open to students, alumni, and lecturers who are members of the College WhatsApp Community. By consenting you confirm that you are at least sixteen (16) years of age or that you have obtained parental/guardian consent to participate.

4. Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may decline to participate or withdraw at any time prior to submission of the questionnaire without penalty, and without explaining the reason. Once the questionnaire is submitted, it may not be possible to withdraw responses if the survey is anonymous.

5. Risks and Discomforts

This study involves minimal risk. Potential discomforts include reflection on negative experiences with group communication or concerns about privacy. You may skip any question you prefer not to answer.

6. Benefits

There is no direct benefit to participants. The findings may inform improvements to communication practices and instructional design at the College, and may contribute to scholarly understanding of mobile messaging in tertiary education.

7. Confidentiality and Data Protection

The questionnaire is designed to collect responses anonymously. No names, telephone numbers, or other direct personal identifiers will be requested. All data will be stored securely on password-protected devices and/or institutional servers. Access to raw data will be limited to the principal investigator and, if required, authorized supervisors. Any quotations used in reports or publications will be anonymized to prevent identification of individuals.

Data will be retained for the period required by institutional policy and applicable research governance (typically a maximum of five years), after which digital files will be securely deleted.

8. Use of Results

Aggregate and anonymized results may be disseminated in academic journals, conferences, and internal College reports. No participant will be identified in any dissemination product.

9. Compensation

There is no financial compensation for participation. If a voluntary incentive (e.g., raffle) is offered, participation details and privacy safeguards will be provided separately.

10. Questions and Contact

If you have questions about the study or your rights as a participant, please contact the principal investigator at your.email@domain.edu.
